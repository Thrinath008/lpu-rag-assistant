TITLE LPU Attendance Policy — Criteria, Detention Rules, and Bonus Provisions

OVERVIEW Lovely Professional University (LPU) enforces a mandatory attendance policy to ensure academic regularity and discipline among students. A minimum of 75% attendance must be maintained in each subject individually, covering both theory and practical sessions, in order to be eligible for End-Term Examinations (ETE). This policy outlines the minimum attendance requirement, consequences of shortfall, available bonus provisions, and the role of duty leaves.

KEY RULES AND POLICY DETAILS

Minimum Attendance Requirement

Students must maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in each subject individually, including both lecture and practical sessions.

Attendance is tracked subject-wise as well as in aggregate for the semester.

Some programs or departments governed by regulatory bodies may have attendance requirements higher than 75%. In such cases, the higher requirement prevails.

Attendance is monitored regularly by faculty and academic departments. Strict action is taken in cases of chronic absenteeism.

Partial Detention

If a student's attendance falls below 75% in one or more subjects but not in three or more subjects, the student is partially detained.

A partially detained student is not permitted to appear in the End-Term Examination (ETE) of the specific subject or subjects in which attendance is below 75%.

The student may still appear for ETEs of all other subjects in which the 75% requirement has been met.

A backlog is recorded for each subject in which the student is detained due to low attendance.

Full Detention

If a student fails to maintain 75% attendance in three or more subjects in a semester, the student is fully detained.

A fully detained student is not permitted to appear in any End-Term Examination for that semester.

The student must re-register for all detained courses and pay the applicable fees again.

BONUS ATTENDANCE PROVISIONS

Previous Semester Bonus (5% Bonus)

If a student's aggregate attendance in the immediately preceding semester was 90% or above, the student is eligible for a 5% bonus attendance in the current semester.

This bonus is applied when the student's current semester attendance falls below 75%, and the previous semester aggregate is verified to be 90% or higher.

First Week Attendance Bonus (10% Bonus)

If a student is present for all classes during the first week of commencement of a new semester, the student is eligible for a 10% bonus attendance for that semester.

Bonus Marks for High Attendance (5 Marks Bonus)

Students who maintain attendance above 90% in a subject are eligible to receive 5 bonus marks in the final result for that subject.

The exact number of bonus marks may vary by subject or department.

DUTY LEAVE (DL)

Duty Leave is an additional provision through which a student's absence from class is condoned if the student was officially present at a university-sanctioned event such as a workshop, seminar, placement drive, or any other authorized university activity during that class period.

Duty Leave is credited to the student's University Management System (UMS) account.

The student is marked as present for the relevant lecture upon credit of the duty leave, ensuring no negative impact on attendance percentage.

IMPORTANT CONDITIONS

Attendance is calculated and displayed subject-wise and in aggregate on the LPU Touch application and UMS interface, allowing students to monitor their standing at any time.

Bonus attendance provisions do not automatically guarantee eligibility for examinations if the shortfall is significant. Students are strongly advised to maintain regular attendance throughout the semester.

Re-registration due to full detention requires payment of full course fees.

The university maintains zero tolerance for chronic absenteeism, and administrative action is initiated promptly when attendance requirements are not being met.

CONTACT AND RESPONSIBLE OFFICE

Faculty Members: Monitor and record attendance for each class session.

Academic Departments and Heads of School (HOS): Track and review attendance records regularly.

Division of Academic Affairs (DAA): Oversees academic policies including attendance eligibility for examinations.

UMS and LPU Touch Application: Students can view their subject-wise and aggregate attendance at any time through these platforms.
