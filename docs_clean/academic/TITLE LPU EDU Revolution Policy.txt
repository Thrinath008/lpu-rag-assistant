TITLE LPU EDU Revolution Policy — Academic Benefits, Grade Upgradation, Duty Leave, and Attendance Benefits

OVERVIEW The EDU Revolution Policy at Lovely Professional University (LPU) is a comprehensive academic benefit framework that rewards students for achievements in research, competitive examinations, internships, certifications, projects, and revenue generation. It provides benefits in the form of attendance relaxation, duty leaves, grade upgradation, course equivalence, and continuous assessment exemptions. All benefits are subject to verification and final approval by the Standing Committee.

KEY RULES AND POLICY DETAILS

General Conditions Applicable to All Benefits

All submitted documents undergo verification by the Standing Committee before any benefit is granted.

No benefit can be claimed as a matter of right. All benefits are subject to approval and may be revised at any time.

Grade upgrade benefits must be claimed before programme completion and before the final marksheet is issued.

Benefits are granted in mapped courses only, as recommended by the Standing Committee.

Academic benefits are granted in a sequential manner based on the stage achieved by the student.

SECTION 1 — 10% ATTENDANCE BENEFIT

Eligibility Conditions

The student must have a minimum CGPA of 7.5 by the end of the term.

The student must maintain a minimum of 60% attendance without any bonus or duty leave by the end of the term.

The student must have no record of indiscipline.

The student must be in the pre-final or final year (for programs longer than 3 years). For programs of 3 years or less, only final-year students are eligible.

What the Benefit Covers

Up to 10% relaxation in attendance requirements for approved academic or professional accomplishments.

Applicable for participation in Recruitment and Competitive Examinations such as GATE, CAT, XAT, MAT, CMAT, GMAT, SNAP, NMAT, GPAT, CLAT, UPSC, State Civil Services, SSB, SSC, AFCAT, CTET, CSIR, UGC NET, ONGC, NTPC, and similar standardized examinations.

Also applicable for any other institutionally approved academic, co-curricular, or professional engagements.

Documents Required

Application Form

Admit Card

Result Card or Answer Key (as and when declared)

Travel Proofs (as per the examination schedule)

Relevant proofs for other approved engagements

Important Note: Attendance benefits must be used for academic purposes that enhance learning, skill development, and overall academic progression.

SECTION 2 — DUTY LEAVE (DL) UNDER EDU REVOLUTION

Definition and Conditions

Duty Leave is the formal approval granted to students for participation in recognized academic, co-curricular, or professional activities conducted during class time.

The student must maintain a minimum of 60% attendance without any bonus or duty leave.

The student must have no record of indiscipline.

Duty Leave is granted only upon final approval of the Standing Committee after verification of all submitted documents.

Categories Eligible for Duty Leave

Recruitment and Competitive Examinations (GATE, CAT, UPSC, SSC, UGC NET, NTPC, etc.)

Duty Leave granted for exam days and travelling days, subject to submission of valid travel proofs.

Maximum 3 days of Duty Leave can be granted.

Not applicable for first-year students.

Technical Competitions and Events (Smart India Hackathon, Microsoft Imagine Cup, RoboCon, Agritech Innovation Challenge, Hult Prize, UPSC CSE, GATE, TCS NQT, DRDO CEPTAM, RRB NTPC, etc.)

Participation must be at National or International level.

The host institution must be NIRF-ranked or a Prime Enterprise or Leading Corporate.

Duty Leave granted for event days and travelling days, subject to valid travel proofs.

Documents Required

Proof of Preparation

Proof of Achievement

Proof of Travelling (if applicable)

SECTION 4 — INTERNSHIP BEYOND CURRICULUM — ACADEMIC BENEFITS

General Conditions

A minimum CGPA of 6.0 is required for internship-based benefits.

In exceptional cases where the internship demonstrates outstanding quality, students with CGPA below 6 may also be considered.

Students are advised to prefer internships during breaks, off-hours, or weekends.

If the internship requires engagement during scheduled class hours, prior approval from the Standing Committee is required to avail additional Duty Leave benefit.

All nominations undergo verification by the Standing Committee.

SECTION 8 — RECOGNITION OF PRIOR LEARNING (RPL)

Eligibility

All active students enrolled in a course are eligible to apply under RPL, provided they submit valid and verifiable proof of prior learning relevant to the mapped course outcomes.

No minimum CGPA requirement is applicable for availing RPL benefits.

Benefit

Complete course waiver for one course per term.

After approval, the student must appear in the RPL examination for the mapped course and secure at least a B+ grade.

Even after exemption, students may appear in the ETE voluntarily to improve their grade.

Important Conditions

Only one course per term is allowed under RPL.

Application must be submitted before the start of the term with valid proof of prior learning.

Suggestive Prior Learning Categories

Professional Certifications: IELTS, TOEFL, DELF/DALF, JLPT, British Council Certifications.

Technical or IT Certifications: AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, CISSP, CEH, CompTIA, SAP, Cisco (CCNA/CCNP), Oracle.

Competitive or Teaching Exams: CTET, TET, TEFL, TESOL, CELTA, Montessori Training.

Work Experience and Internships: Corporate internships, apprenticeships, industrial training.

Online Courses or MOOCs: NPTEL, SWAYAM, Government-approved platforms.

Workshops: Advanced or specialized training programs.

SECTION 9 — STUDENT-CENTRIC REVENUE GENERATION MODEL (SCRGM)

Eligibility: No minimum CGPA requirement for grade upgradation benefits under SCRGM.

Important Conditions

Students must submit the undertaking form while claiming revenue to be generated in one semester. Course equivalence will be processed only after this submission.

Revenue proofs must be submitted at least 15 days before the last teaching day.

Attendance benefit under SCRGM is applicable only to students maintaining a minimum of 60% attendance in that semester without bonus or duty leave.
