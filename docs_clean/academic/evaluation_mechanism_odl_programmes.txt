TITLE LPU Evaluation Mechanism for ODL (Open and Distance Learning) Programmes

OVERVIEW This policy defines the evaluation framework applicable to students enrolled in Open and Distance Learning (ODL) programmes at Lovely Professional University, effective from the academic session 2024–25. It outlines the components of assessment, their respective weightages, the process for assignments and examinations, and the passing criteria required to successfully complete a course and the programme.

KEY RULES AND POLICY DETAILS

Components of Evaluation

All ODL programme courses are evaluated using a combination of the following components:

Continuous Assessment (CA): Based on written assignments submitted by the learner.

End-Term Examination (ETE): Applicable to theory-based courses, conducted as per the university's scheduled examination timetable.

End-Term Practical or Project (ETP): Applicable to practical, project, term paper, dissertation, and Community Development Project (CDP)-based courses.

Evaluation Weightage by Course Category

CONTINUOUS ASSESSMENT (CA) — ASSIGNMENT PROCESS

CA for ODL learners is assessed entirely through assignments.

Assignments are made available to learners through the Learning Management System (LPU e-Connect) and the LPU Touch mobile application.

Learners must download and print the assignment questions, write answers in their own handwriting within the prescribed space, scan the completed assignment, and upload it through the CA submission interface on e-Connect within the stipulated submission schedule.

Assignment Scheme by Course Credit Value

PASSING CRITERIA

Students must meet the following minimum conditions to achieve a pass grade in any course:

Theory Only Course

Minimum 30% marks in ETE, and

Minimum 40% overall when CA and ETE marks are combined.

Theory and Practical Combined Course

Minimum 30% marks separately in both ETE and ETP, and

Minimum 40% overall across all components (CA, ETE, and ETP) combined.

Practical Only Course

Minimum 30% marks in ETP, and

Minimum 40% overall when ETP and CA are combined.

Term Paper, Dissertation, or Project-Based Course

Minimum 40% marks in CA, and

Minimum 40% marks in ETP.

Community Development Project (CDP)

Minimum 40% marks in ETP.

CGPA Requirement for Programme Completion

In addition to passing individual courses, learners must achieve a minimum overall CGPA of 5.0 to successfully complete the programme.

The conversion factor from CGPA to percentage is: CGPA × 10 = Percentage.

CONTACT AND RESPONSIBLE OFFICE

LPU e-Connect (Learning Management System): Platform for accessing and submitting assignments.

LPU Touch Mobile Application: Alternative platform for accessing assignment materials.

Division of Examinations: Responsible for conducting End-Term Examinations as per the official schedule.
