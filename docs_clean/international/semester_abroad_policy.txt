TITLE LPU Semester/Year Abroad Policy

OVERVIEW This policy governs the process by which enrolled full-time students at Lovely Professional University (LPU) may spend a semester or year studying at a foreign, partner, or host university. The policy is applicable to students who have not yet completed their full-time program at LPU. It defines eligibility criteria, types of abroad programs, application procedures, course mapping rules, and post-return academic processes. This policy is effective from July 2018.

KEY RULES AND POLICY DETAILS

Types of Semester/Year Abroad Programs

Scholarship Semester: The student does not pay tuition to the host/foreign university but continues to pay tuition to LPU.

Paid Semester: The student pays tuition to both LPU and the host/foreign university.

Both types are collectively referred to as Semester/Year Abroad.

Minimum Duration at LPU

A student must complete at least 50% of their total program duration at LPU.

Students enrolled in a 1-year program are not eligible for Semester/Year Abroad.

Students in 2-year programs may avail a maximum of 1 term abroad.

Students in programs of 3 years or more may avail a maximum of 1 term or 1 year abroad.

If a regulatory authority imposes stricter conditions for a specific program, those conditions shall take precedence.

Financial Responsibilities

Students are solely responsible for accommodation, living expenses, tuition (where applicable), airfare, visa charges, insurance, and all other related expenses while abroad.

Students remain enrolled at LPU during their time abroad.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA AND REQUIREMENTS

Academic and Disciplinary Standing

The student must be in good academic and disciplinary standing at LPU.

Any pending or current disciplinary issue will be considered during evaluation.

If a student loses academic or disciplinary standing after receiving approval, the approval will be revoked. The student remains responsible for any financial commitments already made, such as processing fees.

General Requirements

The student must have completed at least one full year of study in a regular LPU program before applying.

Once the host university, program, and duration are approved, no changes are permitted after joining the foreign institution.

Document and Financial Requirements

A valid passport with an expiry date at least six months beyond the intended period of study abroad is required.

The student must demonstrate sufficient funds to cover all expenses abroad.

A parent or guardian consent letter must be submitted at the time of application.

Academic Requirements

Minimum CGPA of 6.5 is required.

The student must meet all eligibility requirements set by the host/partner/foreign university.

No re-appears or backlogs are permitted at the time of application. Under exceptional circumstances, a student with up to two re-appears may be considered.

Students must meet the English or other foreign language proficiency requirements of the host university.

PROCEDURE — STEP-BY-STEP APPLICATION AND PROCESS

Step 1 — Expression of Interest and Eligibility Verification

The Division of International Affairs (DIA) uploads the announcement and a Google Form university-wide.

For Autumn Term (Aug–Dec): Announcement uploaded in early August; last date to apply is 30th September.

For Spring Term (Jan–May): Announcement uploaded in early January; last date to apply is 28th February.

Students must review the policy thoroughly and fill out the application form.

A non-refundable processing fee of ₹2,000 must be paid.

Students must submit bank details and a parent's consent letter to DIA.

DIA verifies eligibility of all applicants.

Verification deadline: By 5th March (Autumn) / By 5th October (Spring).

Step 2 — Shortlisting and Interview

DIA shortlists eligible candidates between 1st–5th March (Autumn) or 1st–5th October (Spring).

Shortlisted students appear for an interview conducted by a committee comprising:

Head of School (HOS) of the concerned school

One Head of Department (HOD) nominated by HOS

One senior official from DIA

Students are assessed on: Communication skills, attitude, and awareness of the host university and program.

Interview period: 6th–16th March (Autumn) / 6th–16th October (Spring).

Students must submit an undertaking confirming readiness to bear additional expenses, and agreeing to pay the equivalent of the partner university's tuition fee in case of withdrawal.

Selected students must pay the upcoming term's tuition fee to LPU.

Payment deadline: 17th–22nd March (Autumn) / 17th–22nd October (Spring).

Step 3 — Course Mapping

DIA forwards the host university's scheme and syllabus along with student details to the Division of Academic Affairs (DAA) for course mapping.

Forwarding deadline: 23rd–31st March (Autumn) / 23rd–31st October (Spring).

An Equivalence Committee, comprising one HOD and one senior faculty member nominated by HOS and a DAA nominee, maps host university courses to LPU courses.

Mapping deadline: 23rd March–5th April (Autumn) / 23rd October–5th November (Spring).

Foundation and core courses are mandatory and cannot be waived. Elective requirements may be waived by the committee subject to:

The program not being governed by a regulatory body.

The minimum credit requirement of the program being met after waivers.

Programs governed by a regulatory body must strictly follow the scheme of courses.

Students must fill and submit an undertaking form acknowledging backlogs and waivers.

Course Mapping Rules

Credits of mapped courses passed abroad will be transferred to the student's LPU academic transcript upon return.

LPU courses that are not mapped will be treated as backlogs without payment of backlog fees.

Additional courses studied abroad may be mapped to future LPU term courses, with applicable waivers.

If additional courses studied abroad do not map to any LPU course, a separate certificate will be issued upon the student's special request.

Step 4 — Application Processing

DIA initiates the formal application process to the host/partner/foreign university from 23rd March onwards (Autumn) or 23rd October onwards (Spring).

Students must complete any required English or foreign language proficiency test as required by the host university.

Upon confirmation of application completion, DIA informs DAA to update the student's status to SA (Semester Abroad) or YA (Year Abroad).

Step 5 — Return and Re-enrollment

Upon completing the semester abroad, the student must report to DAA and submit a copy of their official transcript from the host university.

DAA reverts the student's status to Active by coordinating with the Division of Examinations (DOE).

After status update, the student's courses for the upcoming LPU term are registered.

Step 6 — Grade Transfer

The International Student Coordinator, in coordination with DAA and DOE, facilitates the transfer of grades earned abroad to the student's LPU academic transcript.

Grades are converted using an equivalence mapping of the host university's grading system to LPU's grading system.

Step 7 — Late Reporting and Duty Leaves

If a student reports back to LPU after the start of a term due to overlapping semester dates, duty leaves will be updated in the student's account.

A maximum of 45 duty leave days are permitted on a case-by-case basis for overlapping semesters.

Continuous Assessment (CA) is prorated during the duty leave period.

Mid-Term Examination (MTE) marks are prorated based on End-Term Examination (ETE) performance if the student missed MTE due to semester overlap.

DIA will verify the host university's term duration and inform DAA accordingly.

DEADLINES AND IMPORTANT CONDITIONS

Note: If any deadline falls on a university holiday or approved off day, DIA may adjust the dates accordingly.

CONTACT AND RESPONSIBLE OFFICE

Division of International Affairs (DIA): Primary point of contact for all matters related to Semester/Year Abroad applications, eligibility verification, and host university coordination.

Division of Academic Affairs (DAA): Responsible for course mapping, status updates, grade transfers, and re-enrollment upon return.

For any queries regarding this policy, students must visit the Division of International Affairs (DIA) in person.
