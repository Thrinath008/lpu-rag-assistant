TITLE LPU Student Residential and Mess Facility Policy

OVERVIEW Lovely Professional University (LPU) provides on-campus residential and mess facilities to students enrolled in regular programs. This policy outlines the terms and conditions for availing residential accommodation, the types of facilities included, the mess plan options, electricity and maintenance charges, rules of conduct, and the consequences of non-compliance. Residential facility is offered on a first-come-first-served basis and is not a matter of right for any student.

KEY RULES AND POLICY DETAILS

General Conditions

Residential facility is permitted on an annual basis for the duration of the regular academic session.

The university reserves the right to deny residential facility to any student. No student can claim this facility as a matter of right.

Residential facility is allotted on a First Come First Served basis. Priority or reservation may be given to students admitted in residential or specific programs, Honours programs, students from distant locations, or students with exceptional academic, sports, cultural, or co-curricular achievements.

Provisional residential booking is done before the student reports to the university. Final allotment is confirmed only at the time of reporting.

The location, floor, and room pattern of the residential facility are subject to change from time to time.

Duration of Residential Facility

Residential facility is provided until the end of the regular academic session including regular examinations, or for 10 months from the start of the session, whichever is earlier.

Students may join the residential facility two days before the start of the academic session.

Students must vacate the residential room or apartment within two days after the completion of the allotted duration.

For availing residential facility beyond the academic session — for purposes such as summer school, reappear examinations, make-up examinations, PEP, or EEP classes — additional proportionate residential charges must be paid, subject to availability and university permission.

A customized residential plan, such as a full calendar year plan, may be provided on request.

Residential Facility Charges

Residential charges include the cost of accommodation (boarding and lodging), geyser, cooler or air conditioner (as opted), electricity up to the prescribed free units, generator, and maintenance cost for light load.

Charges are applicable for the academic session 2025–26 and are subject to revision for subsequent sessions.

Once a residential facility type is chosen — such as an air-conditioned room — it will not be changed during the academic session, unless otherwise specified.

Electricity consumed in excess of the free units provided by the university will be charged at actual rates. For shared rooms (2-seater, 3-seater, 4-seater), electricity usage is calculated on a per-room basis and divided equally among all occupants.

The electricity meter reading noted on the day prior to the reporting date of the first student allotted to a room is considered the initial reading for all subsequent occupants of that room.

For refund of residential charges, refer to the university's Refund Policy published separately.

RESIDENTIAL FACILITIES AVAILABLE

The following amenities are available to students residing in university accommodation, depending on the room category and pattern allotted:

Separate residential blocks for boys and girls within the campus.

Rooms with attached washrooms (except dormitory), bed with mattress, table, chair, and almirah.

Non-AC or Air Conditioned facility in each room, as opted at the time of admission.

24-hour dedicated power supply through hotlines.

Geyser in each room for hot water during winters, available for scheduled timings (two hours per room per day, or as decided by the university).

On-campus hospital with ambulance for 24-hour medical assistance.

Wi-Fi internet connectivity for scheduled timings for academic purposes only, subject to conditions.

Power backup for light load only. Power backup does not cover AC, geyser, or non-AC units.

Mess facility providing vegetarian meals only.

Free parking for two-wheelers and four-wheelers at designated places within the campus.

Gymnasium facility available on an optional, payment basis.

Permitted electrical items in rooms: Electric Kettle, Electric Iron, Steamer, Laptop, Mobile Charger, Shaving Set, Trimmer, Hair Straightener, and Hair Dryer. Additional electricity charges arising from use of these items are to be borne by the student.

Usage of induction plates is prohibited, except in apartment-type accommodation.

CONDITIONS OF AVAILING RESIDENTIAL FACILITY

Room Occupancy and Shifting

If a student has booked a shared room (e.g., 4-seater) and one or more roommates vacate the room, reducing the occupancy below the allocated room pattern, the university has the right to shift the remaining students to another room with the same pattern to maintain full occupancy. Shifting may or may not be within the same residential block.

If a student wishes to retain the originally allotted room despite reduced occupancy, they will be charged at the rate applicable to the lower occupancy pattern for the remaining period.

Example: If a student is in a 4-seater room and one roommate leaves, reducing occupancy to 3 students, the university may either shift the students to another 4-seater room, or charge each remaining student at the 3-seater rate.

Fee and Refund on Withdrawal

If a student who has availed the residential facility for the full academic session withdraws during the session, no refund of any charges paid will be granted after the last date of refund as per the applicable Refund Policy. All fees and charges paid, including provisional registration amounts, will be forfeited.

If a student seeks to vacate the residential facility due to reasons such as selection for On-Job Training, Internship, Study Abroad Program, or academic exchange programs, they must vacate the allotted room immediately or within the time period stipulated by the university. Refund or adjustment of charges will be processed as per the Refund Policy.

Late Reporting and De-allocation

If a student does not report to the university within 3 days after the scheduled start of the academic session, the allotted residential facility may be de-allocated.

Upon late reporting, a residential facility may be re-allotted subject to availability. However, the room category (standard room or apartment, AC or non-AC) and room pattern (1-seater, 2-seater, 3-seater, 4-seater) may differ from what was originally requested. If the student is not willing to accept the offered category or pattern, the amount deposited may be refunded subject to the applicable Refund Policy.

University Rights

The university reserves the right to evacuate the residential facility partially or fully at any time due to emergency or unforeseen circumstances.

The university may require students to vacate for specific periods to accommodate guests, delegates, event participants, or for maintenance purposes.

The university reserves the right to inspect students' rooms, bags, almirahs, and personal belongings at any time during the day or night.

The university may change the room allotted or modify residential provisions at any time as deemed appropriate.

A student may be barred from applying for residential facility if their CGPA or attendance falls below the prescribed limits, or if a disciplinary case is pending against them.

A student who is detained due to attendance shortage or disciplinary misconduct will be directed to vacate the residential facility. Re-allotment in the next term or semester is entirely at the university's discretion.

RULES OF CONDUCT IN RESIDENTIAL FACILITY

Students residing in the university accommodation must comply with the following rules:

Unauthorized use of electrical gadgets such as heaters, audio players, convectors, and immersion rods is strictly prohibited. Possession of unauthorized items may result in confiscation and imposition of a penalty.

Students must not paste posters, paintings, newspapers, or any material on walls, doors, windows, or cupboards that may damage paint or surfaces.

Students must put thick curtains on windows, ventilators, and doors facing balconies or outside areas.

Damaged CFLs due to the student's default will not be replaced by the university. The student is responsible for replacing them at their own cost.

Hot water through the geyser is available in winter only, during prescribed fixed timings.

Students absent from the residential facility without permission, or who violate any university guidelines, are liable to face disciplinary action including rustication, expulsion, or cancellation of residential facility.

Students are responsible for the cleanliness of their rooms or apartments. Washrooms and corridors are cleaned by housekeeping staff on a periodic basis.

Entry of outsiders, including parents and guardians, into residential accommodation is restricted and subject to the warden's permission. Visitors permitted on campus must behave in an orderly and decent manner. Any misbehavior by a visitor may lead to disciplinary action against the student.

The warden or other competent authority may check on a student's whereabouts and other details at any time as required.

Students are liable to pay for any damage caused to the residential facility, individually or jointly with others.

Students are prohibited from conducting religious indoctrination or group religious observances at any place in the university. Silent, individual worship within the student's own allotted room is permitted, provided it does not disturb other occupants. Loud chants, use of bells, trumpets, cymbals, chimes, or any sound-producing instruments are prohibited. Use of incense sticks, Dhoopbatti, Agarbatti, or any burning material is prohibited within the residential facility due to safety concerns.

If a student is absent from the residential facility without permission or violates university guidelines, the university is entitled to take disciplinary action including rustication, expulsion, or withdrawal of residential facility.

Students must take personal precautions against insect bites, dog bites, snake bites, and similar risks. The university is not liable for such incidents. In case of illness or injury, the student must inform the warden and parents immediately, and is liable to reimburse any expenditure incurred by the university on their treatment.

MESS PLAN

General Conditions

The mess plan is provided until the end of the regular academic session including regular examinations, or for 10 months from the start of the session, whichever is earlier.

Students may avail the mess plan two days before the start of the academic session and must discontinue it within two days after the completion of the allotment duration.

For availing the mess plan beyond the regular academic session — for summer school, reappear examinations, make-up examinations, PEP, or EEP classes — additional proportionate mess charges must be paid, subject to availability.

A customized mess plan, such as a full calendar year plan, may be provided on request.

Mess Options Available

Students may choose to avail the university mess plan or manage their own meals independently.

Flexible options are available, including choice of mess location (within the allocated hostel or at a central location), food variety, and the option to have lunch near the respective academic block, subject to sufficient student demand.

Food Menu Options

Standard Mess: Fixed meal plan providing breakfast, lunch, dinner, and tea with snacks. Unlimited quantity is available for most items.

Ala Carte Menu: Students may choose any combo menu from available cuisine options.

Cuisine Choices: North Indian, South Indian, Rajasthani, Haryanvi, and North-Eastern cuisines are available. Regional cuisines are available at specific locations only.

All meals served in the mess are vegetarian only.

Charges for the mess plan are applicable for the academic session 2025–26 and are subject to revision for subsequent sessions.

The university strongly advises students to opt for on-campus residential facility at the time of admission to avoid unavailability at a later stage. The following advantages of on-campus residence over privately rented accommodation or paying guest (PG) facilities are noted:

Zero tolerance for ragging, alcohol, smoking, drugs, and disciplinary misconduct within the campus.

24×7×365 security with CCTV coverage at all prominent locations. Automated turnstile gates and biometric machines regulate entry to residential facilities.

Online leave management system allowing parents to be informed of their ward's leave status and movement through SMS.

Hygienic, nutritious, multi-cuisine mess served four times a day. Automatic chapatti-making machines are available in every residential accommodation.

30-bedded on-campus hospital with 24-hour medical and ambulance services staffed by qualified medical professionals.

Library accessible from 8:00 AM to midnight. Lab and workshop facilities also available during extended hours.

Opportunity for group study with peers from the same program or department.

24-hour dedicated power supply with power backup for light load.

Branches of 5 nationalized banks and 55+ ATMs within the campus.

A dedicated Post Office within the campus for money transfer, speed post, and related services.

Wi-Fi internet connectivity through a 650 Mbps leased line, along with intranet access for UMS, academic records, and online study material.

Laundry services available within residential accommodation at nominal charges.

On-campus shopping mall (Uni Mall) with departmental stores, food courts, bookshops, and entertainment options.

Over 30 restaurants and 80+ shops and kiosks within the campus.

No commuting time or transportation cost, as the residential facility is within the campus.

Part-time job opportunities in labs, library, sales, and the shopping mall available exclusively to students residing on campus.

Cultural and recreational activities organized regularly by the Division of Student Welfare (DSW).

Indoor and outdoor sports facilities. Gym or sports facility is available at a subsidized fee of ₹5,000 or ₹2,500 (depending on the sport) for residential students, compared to ₹6,000 for non-residential students.

Access to 500+ faculty and staff residing on campus for academic or personal assistance.
